A cannabis retail store licence allows you to sell non-medical cannabis and cannabis accessories in British Columbia.

Below is a step-by-step guide to applying for a cannabis retail store licence and selling your products.

 

 | 
1 Register your business
  • You need to have a business that is registered in B.C.
  • Your business must be registered to collect PST.
  • Your business must have a Business Number (9-digit number issued by the Canada Revenue Agency)
 2 Create a Business BCeID
  • Your business will need a Business BCeID to log in.
  • Your business can have only one Business BCeID, but you should create separate accounts for each person using it.
 3Find a location for your store
  • Contact the local government or Indigenous Nation to make sure they are accepting applications for cannabis retail stores in their community.
  • Ask about the local requirements for zoning, bylaws, business licences and cannabis stores.
  • You must own, lease, or have an agreement to lease the location.
 4Submit your application

Your application requires:

  • Information about your business.
  • Information about your proposed location.
  • Consent for security clearance and financial integrity checks.
  • A declaration of financial, business or family connections to any licensed producers.
  • An application fee.

You must apply for a separate licence for each store.

 5 Get local approval

Your storeâ€™s location is under the jurisdiction of either a local government or an Indigenous Nation.

Local Governments

  • If your store is located under the jurisdiction of a local government, your application must be approved by the local government before you can be licensed.
  • Local governments must hold a public consultation with nearby residents.
  • Ask the local government about their approval or zoning requirements.

Indigenous Nations

  • If your store is located under the jurisdiction of an Indigenous Nation, your application must be approved by the Indigenous Nation before you can be licensed.
  • Indigenous Nations must hold a public consultation with nearby residents. They can use the provincial process for local government consultations or develop a process of their own.
  • Ask the Indigenous Nation about their approval or zoning requirements.
 6 Receive conditional approval

If your application meets local and provincial requirements, you will get conditional approval from the province. Conditional approval means you will receive your provincial retail licence after passing a final inspection.

Now you can:

  • Secure ownership or a lease of your store.
  • Make sure you meet the terms and conditions of your licence.
  • Make changes to your store to match the description you submitted in your application, including:
    • Floor plan
    • Exterior signage
    • Window covering

The province does not know how many applications for a cannabis retail store licence will be received. Therefore, it is difficult to predict how long applications will take to process at this time.

 7 Pass a store inspection
  • The inspection ensures your store meets provincial regulation.
  • You can schedule an inspection when you are ready.

You cannot have any cannabis in your store at the time of the inspection.

 8 Receive your licence
  • You will receive your licence after passing your inspection and paying a first-year licensing fee.
  • You must display your licence in a prominent place in your store.
 9 Register and train your staff
  • Your staff must be registered with the province before they can work in your store.
  • More information about registration and training will be available soon.
 10 Order cannabis products
  • Before you can order cannabis products, you must register with the Liquor Distribution Branch.
  • You can only sell products that you purchase from the Liquor Distribution Branch.
  • You need your licence number to place an order.
 11 Report your sales
  • You need to report your inventory and sales on a regular basis.
