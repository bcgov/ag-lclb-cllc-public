A cannabis retail store licence allows licensees to sell non-medical cannabis and cannabis accessories in British Columbia.

Below is a step-by-step guide to applying for a non-medical cannabis retail store licence and selling cannabis products.

 

Individuals (sole proprietors), partnerships, corporations and Indigenous nations may apply for a cannabis retail store licence.

 | 
1 Register the business
  • All applicants must be registered in B.C. as a sole proprietor, partnership or corporation.
  • Indigenous nation applicants may apply either as one of the above type of legal entity or in their own right.
  • All applicants must be registered to collect PST.
  • All applicants must have a Business Number (9-digit number issued by the Canada Revenue Agency)
 2 Create a Business BCeID
  • All applicants will need a Business BCeID to log in.
  • Applicants can have only one Business BCeID but should create separate accounts for each person using it.
 3Find a store location
  • Applicants must own, lease, or have an agreement to lease the proposed retail store location before applying.
 4Submit an application

This application requires:

  • Information about the applicant and associates (including, but not necessarily limited to, directors, shareholders (direct and indirect) and partners)
  • Information about the proposed location.
  • Information about other licences held by the applicant or other applications for licences from the applicant.
  • Consent for security screening and financial integrity checks.
  • A declaration of financial, business or family connections to any licensed cannabis producers and retail licence holders or applicants for retail licences.
  • An application fee.

Applicants must apply for a separate licence for each store.

 5 Get local government or Indigenous nation recommendation
  • If a proposed store is located in the area of a local government or Indigenous nation, the Liquor Control and Licensing Branch must provide notice of the application to the local government or Indigenous nation.
  • A local government or Indigenous nation that wishes to make a recommendation to the Liquor Control and Licensing Branch in response to a notice of application must gather the views of residents and must provide the comments and recommendation to the Liquor Control and Licensing Branch.

The Liquor Control and Licensing Branch will take the recommendation into account when deciding whether to issue a licence.

 6 Conditional approval for a licence

If conditional approval is given, the applicant can:

  • Secure ownership or a lease for the store.
  • Make sure the terms and conditions of the licence are met.
  • Make changes to the store to match the description submitted with the application, including:
    • Floor plan
    • Exterior signage
    • Window covering

The province does not know at this time how many applications for non-medical cannabis retail store licences will be received or how long the local government or Indigenous nation public recommendation process will take. Therefore, it is difficult to predict how long applications will take to process.

7 Pass a store inspection
  • Liquor Control and Licensing Branch inspectors will assess whether the store meets provincial regulation and any requirements established by the applicable local government or Indigenous nation.
  • Prospective licensees can schedule their inspection when ready.

The applicant will not be authorized to possess cannabis until the licence application has been approved. There must be no illicit cannabis in the proposed retail location at the time of the inspection.

 8 Receive a licence
  • Successful applicants will receive a licence after the Liquor Control and Licensing Branch has confirmed that all licence requirements have been met, the inspection has been passed and the licensing fee has been received by government.
  • Licensees must display the licence in a prominent place in their store.
 9 Register and train employees
  • All employees must be registered with the province before they can be employed in a cannabis store. Local governments may also have business licence requirements regarding employees.
  • More information about registration and training will be available soon.
 10 Order cannabis products
  • Before a licensee can order cannabis products, they must register with the Liquor Distribution Branch.
  • Licensees can only sell products purchased from the Liquor Distribution Branch.
  • Licensees will need a licence number to place an order.
 11 Report your sales
  • Licensees need to report inventory and sales on a regular basis.
