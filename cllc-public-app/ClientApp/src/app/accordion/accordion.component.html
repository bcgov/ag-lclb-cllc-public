A cannabis retail store licence allows licensees to sell non-medical cannabis and cannabis accessories in British Columbia.

Below is a step-by-step guide to applying for a cannabis retail store licence and selling cannabis products.

 

 | 
1 Register the business
  • Applicants must have a business that is registered in B.C.
  • Applicants must be registered to collect PST.
  • Applicants must have a Business Number (9-digit number issued by the Canada Revenue Agency)
 2 Create a Business BCeID
  • Applicants will need a Business BCeID to log in.
  • Applicants can have only one Business BCeID but should create separate accounts for each person using it.
 3Find a store location
  • Contact the local government or Indigenous Nation to make sure they are accepting applications for cannabis retail stores in their community.
  • Ask about the local requirements for zoning, bylaws, business licences and cannabis stores.
  • Applicants must own, lease, or have an agreement to lease the location.
 4Submit an application

This application requires:

  • Information about the proposed business.
  • Information about the proposed location.
  • Consent for security clearance and financial integrity checks.
  • A declaration of financial, business or family connections to any licensed producers.
  • An application fee.

Applicants must apply for a separate licence for each store.

 5 Get local approval

A storeâ€™s location is under the jurisdiction of either a local government or an Indigenous Nation.

Local Governments

  • If a store is located under the jurisdiction of a local government, the application must be approved by the local government before a licence will be issued.
  • Local governments must hold a public consultation with nearby residents.
  • Ask the local government about their approval or zoning requirements.

Indigenous Nations

  • If a store is located under the jurisdiction of an Indigenous Nation, the application must be approved by the Indigenous Nation before a licence will be issued.
  • Indigenous Nations must hold a public consultation with nearby residents. They can use the provincial process for local government consultations or develop a process of their own.
  • Ask the Indigenous Nation about their approval or zoning requirements.
 6 Receive conditional approval

If an application meets local and provincial requirements, the applicant will receive conditional approval from the province.

The applicant can now:

  • Secure ownership or a lease for the store.
  • Make sure the terms and conditions of the licence are met.
  • Make changes to the store to match the description submitted with the application, including:
    • Floor plan
    • Exterior signage
    • Window covering

The province does not know how many applications for a cannabis retail store licence will be received. Therefore, it is difficult to predict how long applications will take to process at this time.

 7 Pass a store inspection
  • The inspection ensures the store meets provincial regulation.
  • Prospective licensees can schedule an inspection when ready.

You cannot have any cannabis in the store at the time of the inspection.

 8 Receive a licence
  • Successful applicants will receive a licence after passing an inspection and paying a first-year licensing fee.
  • Licensees must display the licence in a prominent place in their store.
 9 Register and train employees
  • All employees must be registered with the province before they can work in a cannabis store.
  • More information about registration and training will be available soon.
 10 Order cannabis products
  • Before a licensee can order cannabis products, they must register with the Liquor Distribution Branch.
  • Licensees can only sell products purchased from the Liquor Distribution Branch.
  • Licensees will need a licence number to place an order.
 11 Report your sales
  • Licensees need to report inventory and sales on a regular basis.
