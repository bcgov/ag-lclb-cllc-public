Selling Cannabis Step-by-Step

 

1 Register a Business
  • You must have a registered business or another legal entity to apply for a cannabis retail licence.
  • The province will review and approve the name of your business. It can not contain words like pharmacy, apothecary, or dispensary to suggest your business sells medical cannabis.

Read more about  registering a business for cannabis

 2 Create a Business BCeID Account
  • A Business BCeID account is required to login to the application.
  • It takes about 15 minutes to complete the 5 step registration process. To begin, visit Register for a Business BCeID

Read more about  Business BCeID

 3 Contact Your Local Government or First Nation
  • It's up to local governments and First Nations to decide if they'll allow cannabis retail stores.
  • Be sure to check with your local authority before applying. The province will not issue a cannabis retail licence in a community that doesn't support it.
  • Local government or First Nation support does not mean your business has a local business licence or that it has been zoned for cannabis sales. Check with your local authority about what to do next.

    • Read more about local government and First Nation involvement

 4 Submit a Licence Application
    You must:
  • Submit information about your business
  • Submit a detailed floor plan
  • Authorize background checks to be conducted
  • Declare any financial or business connections to licensed producers
    Cost & Fees
  • $xxx application fee

  • $xxx yearly licence fee (Paid after approval)
 5 Public Consultation
  • All cannabis stores, including stores run by the province, require a public consultation.
  • Local governments and First Nations decide the area around the store to consult.
  • These governments decide specific restrictions on stores such as:
    • Distance from schools and community centres
    • Distance from other cannabis stores
    • Hours of operations
    • Any specific conditions the store must adhere to
  • A public consultation may be used by these governments to provide local business licence and proper zoning for cannabis sales. Check with your local government about what they require.

Read more about the public consultation process

 6 Receive Background Checks
  • A background check is a review of a person's history. It can include an examination of their criminal, police, financial and other records.
  • The type of business applying for a license will determine who requires a background check.
  • You will authorize a background check to be conducted as part of your application.
  • A $xxxx deposit will be required to cover the cost of checks. You will be refunded any unused amounts.

Read more about background checks

 7 Inspection of Store Location
  • A store inspection is required before you are issued a licence.
  • An inspection is done to ensure the information about your store matches the information provided during the application.
  • If you currently operate a cannabis store, you must not have any cannabis at the location at the time of inspection.

Read more about getting an inspection

 8 Receive a Licence
  • A licence will be emailed to you after an inspection is completed and you have completed all the required local government or First Nation requirements.
 9 Order Cannabis Product
  • You can order cannabis products from the provincial distributor after you receive a licence.
  • Only products ordered from the provincial distributor can be sold.

Read more about ordering cannabis products

 10 Train Employees
  • Staff must complete the Selling it Right training before selling cannabis.
  • Selling it Right is a program that helps staff of cannabis stores sell cannabis responsibly
